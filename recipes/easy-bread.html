Easy Artisan Bread

Back to Home Page

Description

This recipe takes very little prep time and makes amazing artisan bread loaves. If you want to be a bit more adventurous, try adding herbs to the bread or sprinkling something on top of the loaf before baking.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups lukewarm water
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons yeast
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons salt
  • 6 1/2 cups all purpose flour
  • Cornmeal, for underneath the loaves

Steps

  1. Make sure the water is lukewarm. Technically, this recipe will work with cold water, but you'll need significantly more rising time - 4 or 5 hours at least.
  2. Add yeast and salt to the water in a large (at least 5 quart) bowl or food-grade bucket, preferably with a lid. Don't worry about dissolving everything.
  3. Mix in the flour all at once. You'll want to measure the flour by scooping and sweeping the excess off the top; don't compact the flour. You can mix with a wooden spoon, food processor with at least 14 cups capacity, or a sturdy stand mixer with a dough hook. I use a wooden spoon, personally.
  4. Once the dough is uniformally moist, without dry patches, it's time to let it rise. Cover the dough with a lid or a towel, and make sure the lid isn't airtight - using a sealed jar or similar could result in a small explosion, and no one wants to clean dough and broken glass off the walls.
  5. Allow dough to rise at room temperature until it begins to collapse or flatten at the top, generally around two hours. You can allow it to rise up to five hours without compramising the recipe. After the dough rises, you can refridgerate it until you're ready to bake.
  6. Cloak and shape the dough, no kneeding necesssary. First, you want to prep a "pizza peal" - sprinkle cornmeal on the baking surface. Then, with floured hands, shape about a pound of dough into each loaf, "cloaking" the loaf by stretching the surface of the dough around to the bottom on all sides. The seam produced by cloaking should be facing down on your baking surface.
  7. Allow the dough to rest for about 40 minutes, and preheat the oven to 450Â°F about 20 minutes before baking.
  8. When it's time to bake, sprinkle flour on the dough and slash the top of each loaf 2-4 times with the tip of a very sharp knife. The slashes should be about 1/4 inch deep.
  9. When the oven is preheated, put an oven-safe bowl of water on the lower rack of the oven. This will produce steam as the loaves bake. Place the baking surface with the loaves on the middle rack of the oven.
  10. Bake the loaves for about 30 minutes, or until the crust is nice and brown and firm to the touch. You can also knock on a loaf and listen for a sort of hollow sound to be sure it's baked through. Remove from the oven and allow to cool before serving.
  11. If you have leftover dough, you can store it in the fridge for up to 14 days.
